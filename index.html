About Me

Hi I’m Christina. I recently graduated from the University of Washington with a Bachelor of Arts Degree in Technical Communication and a minor in Applied Computing. During my sophomore year of college, I fell in love with transforming the world through inclusive design practices when I worked on my first usability test for Allrecipes.com. From that moment on, my mind has been focused on how we can change the world by creating meaningful user experiences for diverse user groups.

I have spent the last year and a half working on Project EMAR at the University of Washington. Project EMAR is a human-centered design project funded by the National Science Foundation to create a social robot that will address teen stress. As part of this project, I volunteer in local high schools teaching human-centered design best practices to teens.



When I’m not thinking about designing social robots, you can find me out on the river fishing with my family, or up in the mountains riding (and often, falling off) my mountain bike.

I would love to get in touch with you, so if you have any questions, please reach out!
