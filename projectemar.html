Project EMAR

Overview

Project EMAR is an interdisciplinary project at the University of Washington to develop an autonomous social robot that will capture adolescent stress levels in a local high school. The project is currently funded by the National Science Foundation as part of the National Robotics Initiative.

The Team

Project EMAR is an interdisciplinary team at UW consisting of faculty, staff, graduate and undergraduate students. Our team also collaborates with a diverse range of teens at high schools in the Pacific Northwest.

Method

Our team uses the human-centered design process to develop our social robot. We also use participatory design strategies, working with teens in local high schools to develop low-fidelity social robot porototypes that they can envision living in their own school. After working with teens, we use the ideas that teens came up with in the field as an inspiration for our design. This is an open source project and we share all our work publicly.
